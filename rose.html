Hyssop Herb / Zofa

Code: P-265
English Name: Hyssop Herb / Zofa
Botanical Name: Hyssopus Officinalis
Local Name: Zofa
Chinese Name: 牛膝草

Product Description

Hyssop / Gul Zofa also known as Hyssopus Officinalis, has been used for cleansing ritual and as medicine since biblical times. Today it is known that the volatile oil and other ingredients that are contained in the leaves and green stems indeed have antibacterial, antiviral and other beneficial properties.
Hyssop contains several compounds that are also conductive to positive responses in the human body. Hyssop helps reduce the swelling of a wound or the effects of illness by constricting blood vessels. This constriction also slows excessive bleeding and causes the body to produce fewer wrinkles by tightening the skin. It is also helpful to joints affected by arthritis and improves circulation to the afflicted areas

Water Lilly Flower / Nilofar

English Name: Water Lilly Flower
Botanical Name: Nymphaea nouchali
Local Name: Nilofar

Product Description

The water lily, also known as Nymphaea, is a graceful and enchanting aquatic plant that floats atop the serene surfaces of ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Characterized by its exquisite beauty and elegant design, the water lily is celebrated for its delicate, cup-shaped blossoms and lush, round leaves.
These enchanting flowers come in a variety of colors, including shades of white, pink, yellow, and blue, each hue adding to the allure of this aquatic marvel. The blossoms, often fragrant and waxy in texture, open in the morning and close in the late afternoon, creating a mesmerizing display of nature’s cyclical beauty.

Lavender / Ustakhdoos

English Name: Lavender
Botanical Name: Lavandula Spica
Local Name: Ustakhdoos

Product Description

Lavender, scientifically known as Lavandula, is a fragrant herb widely recognized for its soothing aroma and versatile uses. With its distinctive purple flowers and silvery-green foliage, lavender is not only visually appealing but also renowned for its calming properties. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and is cultivated globally for various purposes, including culinary, medicinal, and ornamental uses.
In aromatherapy, lavender essential oil is cherished for its calming and relaxing effects, often used to alleviate stress, anxiety, and insomnia. It is a popular ingredient in perfumes, soaps, and candles due to its pleasant scent. Culinary enthusiasts appreciate lavender for its subtle floral flavor, which enhances both sweet and savory dishes
